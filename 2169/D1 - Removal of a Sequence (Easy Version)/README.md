The difference between the versions is the constraint on $$$x$$$; in this version, $$$x \le 10^5$$$.</span></p><p>Polycarp has a sequence of all natural numbers from $$$1$$$ to $$$10^{12}$$$. He decides to modify this sequence by performing the following action $$$x$$$ times:</p><ul> <li> Simultaneously remove all numbers at positions $$$y$$$, $$$2 \cdot y$$$, $$$3 \cdot y$$$, ..., $$$m \cdot y \le n$$$, where $$$n$$$ is the length of the current sequence. </li></ul><p>After that, Polycarp wants to find the $$$k$$$-th number in the remaining sequence or determine that the length of the resulting sequence is less than $$$k$$$. </p><p>Help Polycarp solve this problem!</p><p>Consider an example. Let $$$x = 2$$$, $$$y = 3$$$, $$$k = 5$$$, then:</p><center> <img class="tex-graphics" src="https://espresso.codeforces.com/d72e39099c3d7824b722e3747c0a15a60a44a6c9.png" style="zoom: 50.0%;max-width: 100.0%;max-height: 100.0%;"> </center><p>The numbers crossed out with a red line were removed after the first operation, and the numbers crossed out with a blue line were removed after the second operation. Thus, the number at position $$$k = 5$$$ is the number $$$10$$$.</p></div><div class="input-specification"><div class="section-title">Input</div><p>Each test contains multiple test cases. 10^{12}$$$).</p></div><div class="output-specification"><div class="section-title">Output</div><p>For each test case, output a positive integer that is at the $$$k$$$-th position in the resulting
